Why MCP changes everything for knowledge creators

A playbook for turning your notes into an asset that earns while you think.

For the last five years, I've watched tools promise "your second brain" and deliver another silo. Notion, Roam, Obsidian — beautiful, but locked inside apps and behind screens.

MCP flips that. Your knowledge becomes a queryable surface. You stop being the reader of your own notes and start being someone whose thinking can be queried, on demand, by agents working for you — or for people who subscribe to your House.

Three implications I keep coming back to:

1. The unit of value shifts from "the document" to "the ability to answer." No one pays for raw notes. They pay for a mind that answered their question.

2. Embeddings + quality scoring let us separate signal from volume. Prolific ≠ valuable. A tightly-reasoned 500-word post can outrank 50 half-finished pages.
