For the last five years, I've watched tools promise "your second brain" and deliver another silo. Notion, Roam, Obsidian — beautiful, but locked inside apps and behind screens. MCP flips that. Your knowledge becomes a queryable surface. You stop being the reader of your own notes and start being someone whose thinking can be queried, on demand, by agents working for you — or for people who subscribe to your House. Three implications I keep coming back to: 1. The unit of value shifts from "the document" to "the ability to answer." No one pays for raw notes. They pay for a mind that answered their question. 2. Embeddings + quality scoring let us separate signal from volume. Prolific ≠ valuable. A tightly-reasoned 500-word post can outrank 50 half-finished pages. 3. Subscription access > content sale. You're not selling what you wrote; you're selling the inference layer on top of it. Ratel is building toward this. Connect Notion, open a House, let your essence compound.
