Get your app password
in under a minute.

Bluesky uses app passwords instead of OAuth. You'll generate one on Bluesky, paste it back into Ratel, and that's it — we'll never see your main password.

4 steps ~60 seconds Revocable anytime
How to do it 4 steps
1

Open Bluesky's app passwords page

Sign in to Bluesky first (in any browser tab) and then visit the App Passwords page in your account settings.

https://bsky.app/settings/app-passwords
Settings · Privacy & Security · App Passwords
2

Tap "Add App Password" and name it

Bluesky will ask for a short label so you can revoke this specific token later without affecting your other apps. Name it Ratel so it's easy to spot.

bsky.app · App Password Name
Ratel Create
Any label is fine — but a clear name (e.g. Ratel) makes it easy to find and revoke later in Bluesky's settings.
3

Copy the generated password

Bluesky will show you a fresh password in the xxxx-xxxx-xxxx-xxxx format. Copy it right away — Bluesky only displays it once.

Save it before closing the tab. If you lose it you can't view it again — just revoke and create a new one.
4

Come back here and paste it in

Enter your handle (without the @) and the app password you just copied. Ratel encrypts and stores it — you can disconnect anytime.

Why Ratel needs this

Your main password stays yours

Encrypted at rest Stored with KMS-grade encryption. Ratel never sees your main Bluesky password.
Revocable anytime Revoke from Bluesky settings or disconnect inside Ratel — both work instantly.
Used only for posting Ratel uses this token to syndicate your Ratel posts to your Bluesky timeline — nothing else.
Scoped by Bluesky App passwords can't change your email, password, or delete your account — Bluesky enforces this.
